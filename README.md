<h1>Description</h1>
This project evaluates the economic impact of recurrent flooding in Lodi, New Jersey, and assesses the feasibility of using permeable pavement (specifically porous asphalt) as a sustainable flood-mitigation strategy. Drawing on historical flood data, community demographics, and a detailed survey of residents, the study quantifies both the rising frequency and financial costs of flood events and measures local willingness to pay for resiliency solutions. By mapping flood-prone zones, identifying 14 strategic installation sites, and conducting a cost-benefit analysis, the research demonstrates that installing permeable pavement in just 10% of affected areas could reduce long-term flood damages by more than $1.5 billion, far outweighing the estimated $37 million installation cost. Survey findings reveal moderate community support, with most residents willing to contribute up to $100 annually, though many require evidence of proven success before committing. Overall, the project illustrates how targeted green infrastructure investments can significantly strengthen Lodiâ€™s resilience while offering an economically viable pathway to long-term flood reduction.
